Naive Bayes Classifier

In statistics, naive Bayes classifiers are a family of simple "probabilistic classifiers" based on applying Bayes' theorem with strong (naive) independence assumptions between the features

Naive Bayes classifiers are highly scalable, requiring a number of parameters linear in the number of variables (features/predictors) in a learning problem.

Naive Bayes classifiers are often extremely fast compared to more sophisticated methods.

Naive Bayes classifiers have very low memory requirements.

They can be extremely useful even if their assumptions donâ€™t hold, which is very common in practice.

Naive Bayes classifiers are often very easily interpretable, providing a nice probability distribution over the classes for each input.

Naive Bayes classifiers can be very easily updated, which makes it possible to stream data.

Naive Bayes classifiers are often very good in practice, even if their theoretical justification for why they work is shaky.
